To solve the equation 

\[
1 - \sin^4(x) - \cos^2(x) = \frac{1}{16}
\]

we will follow the steps outlined previously and synthesize the results.

### Step 1: Rearranging the Equation

Starting with the original equation:

\[
1 - \sin^4(x) - \cos^2(x) = \frac{1}{16}
\]

We rearranged it to isolate \(\sin^4(x)\) and \(\cos^2(x)\):

\[
\sin^4(x) + \cos^2(x) = \frac{15}{16}
\]

### Step 2: Substitute \(\cos^2(x)\)

Using the identity \(\cos^2(x) = 1 - \sin^2(x)\), we substitute into the equation:

\[
\sin^4(x) + (1 - \sin^2(x)) = \frac{15}{16}
\]

This simplifies to:

\[
\sin^4(x) - \sin^2(x) + 1 = \frac{15}{16}
\]

### Step 3: Rearranging Further

Subtract \(\frac{15}{16}\) from both sides:

\[
\sin^4(x) - \sin^2(x) + 1 - \frac{15}{16} = 0
\]

This simplifies to:

\[
\sin^4(x) - \sin^2(x) + \frac{1}{16} = 0
\]

### Step 4: Letting \(y = \sin^2(x)\)

Let \(y = \sin^2(x)\). Then the equation becomes:

\[
y^2 - y + \frac{1}{16} = 0
\]

### Step 5: Solving the Quadratic Equation

Using the quadratic formula \(y = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\):

\[
y = \frac{-(-1) \pm \sqrt{(-1)^2 - 4 \cdot 1 \cdot \frac{1}{16}}}{2 \cdot 1}

\[
y = \frac{1 \pm \sqrt{1 - \frac{1}{4}}}{2}

\[
y = \frac{1 \pm \sqrt{\frac{3}{4}}}{2}

\[
y = \frac{1 \pm \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}}{2}

\[
y = \frac{2 \pm \sqrt{3}}{4}

### Step 6: Finding \(\sin^2(x)\)
