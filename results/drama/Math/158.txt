### Step 1: Calculate the Area of Circle \( P \)

1. **Determine the radius**: 
   Given that segment \( AB \) measures 4 cm and is the diameter of circle \( P \), we can find the radius \( r \):
   \[
   r = \frac{AB}{2} = \frac{4 \text{ cm}}{2} = 2 \text{ cm}
   \]

2. **Calculate the area of circle \( P \)**:
   Using the formula for the area of a circle \( A = \pi r^2 \):
   \[
   A = \pi (2 \text{ cm})^2 = \pi \cdot 4 \text{ cm}^2 = 4\pi \text{ cm}^2
   \]

### Step 2: Calculate the Area of Triangle \( ABC \)

   - Let \( A(-2, 0) \) and \( B(2, 0) \) be the endpoints of the diameter on the x-axis.
   - Point \( C \) is on the circle \( P \) and is directly below the center at \( P(0, 0) \) at a distance equal to the radius, so \( C(0, -2) \).

2. **Calculate the lengths of the sides of triangle \( ABC \)**:
   - \( AB = 4 \text{ cm} \) (the diameter)
   - \( BC = 2 \text{ cm} \) (given)
   - To find \( AC \), we can use the distance formula:
     \[
     AC = \sqrt{(0 - (-2))^2 + (-2 - 0)^2} = \sqrt{(2)^2 + (-2)^2} = \sqrt{4 + 4} = \sqrt{8} = 2\sqrt{2} \text{ cm}
     \]

3. **Calculate the area of triangle \( ABC \)**:
   We can use the formula for the area of a triangle given its vertices:
   \[
   \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \left| x_1(y_2 - y_3) + x_2(y_3 - y_1) + x_3(y_1 - y_2) \right|
   \]
   Substituting the coordinates \( A(-2, 0) \), \( B(2, 0) \), and \( C(0, -2) \):
   \[
   \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \left| -2(0 - (-2)) + 2(-2 - 0) + 0(0 - 0) \right|
   \]
   \[
   = \frac{1}{2} \left| -2(2) + 2(-2) + 0 \right| = \frac{1}{2} \left| -4 - 4 \right| = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 8 = 4 \text{ cm}^2
   \]

### Step 3: Calculate the Area of the Shaded Region

Now that we have both areas, we can find the area of the shaded region:
\[
\text{Area of shaded region} = \text{Area of circle } P - \text{Area of triangle } ABC

\[
