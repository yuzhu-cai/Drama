To evaluate the expression \(\left|\dfrac{2-4i}{2+i}\right|\), we can use the property of moduli of complex numbers, which states that:

\[
\left|\dfrac{z_1}{z_2}\right| = \frac{|z_1|}{|z_2|}
\]

where \(z_1\) and \(z_2\) are complex numbers. In this case, \(z_1 = 2 - 4i\) and \(z_2 = 2 + i\).

### Step 1: Calculate the modulus of \(2 - 4i\)

Using the formula for the modulus of a complex number:

\[
|z| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}
\]

For \(2 - 4i\):
- Real part \(a = 2\)
- Imaginary part \(b = -4\)

Calculating the modulus:

\[
|2 - 4i| = \sqrt{2^2 + (-4)^2} = \sqrt{4 + 16} = \sqrt{20} = 2\sqrt{5}
\]

### Step 2: Calculate the modulus of \(2 + i\)

For \(2 + i\):
- Real part \(a = 2\)
- Imaginary part \(b = 1\)

Calculating the modulus:

\[
|2 + i| = \sqrt{2^2 + 1^2} = \sqrt{4 + 1} = \sqrt{5}
\]

### Step 3: Divide the moduli

Now we can find the modulus of the quotient:

\[
\left|\dfrac{2-4i}{2+i}\right| = \frac{|2 - 4i|}{|2 + i|} = \frac{2\sqrt{5}}{\sqrt{5}}
\]

### Step 4: Simplify the result

The \(\sqrt{5}\) in the numerator and denominator cancels out:

\[
\frac{2\sqrt{5}}{\sqrt{5}} = 2
\]

### Final Result

Thus, the comprehensive solution to the original query is:

\[
\left|\dfrac{2-4i}{2+i}\right| = \boxed{2}
\]
