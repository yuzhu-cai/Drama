To determine the sum of all single-digit replacements for \( n \) such that the number \( 42789n37 \) is divisible by 3, we follow these steps:

### Step 1: Apply the Divisibility Rule for 3
The rule states that a number is divisible by 3 if the sum of its digits is divisible by 3. 

### Step 2: Calculate the Sum of Known Digits
The digits in \( 42789n37 \) are \( 4, 2, 7, 8, 9, n, 3, 7 \). We first calculate the sum of the known digits, excluding \( n \):

\[
4 + 2 + 7 + 8 + 9 + 3 + 7 = 40
\]

### Step 3: Include \( n \) in the Sum
Now, we include \( n \) in the total sum:

\[
\text{Total Sum} = 40 + n
\]

### Step 4: Set Up the Divisibility Condition
For \( 42789n37 \) to be divisible by 3, the total sum \( 40 + n \) must be divisible by 3:

\[
40 + n \equiv 0 \mod 3
\]

### Step 5: Calculate \( 40 \mod 3 \)
Next, we find the remainder of \( 40 \) when divided by 3:

\[
40 \div 3 = 13 \quad \text{(remainder } 1\text{)}
\]

Thus, we have:

\[
40 \equiv 1 \mod 3
\]

### Step 6: Solve for \( n \)
We need:

\[
1 + n \equiv 0 \mod 3
\]

This simplifies to:

\[
n \equiv -1 \mod 3 \quad \text{or} \quad n \equiv 2 \mod 3
\]

### Step 7: Identify Valid Single-Digit Values for \( n \)
The single-digit values for \( n \) that satisfy \( n \equiv 2 \mod 3 \) are:

- \( n = 2 \)
- \( n = 5 \)
- \( n = 8 \)

### Step 8: Calculate the Sum of Valid Values
Now, we sum these valid values of \( n \):

\[
2 + 5 + 8 = 15
\]

The sum of all single-digit replacements for \( n \) such that the number \( 42789n37 \) is divisible by 3 is:

\[
\boxed{15}
