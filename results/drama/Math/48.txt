To evaluate \(\sqrt{5 \cdot 10 \cdot 14 \cdot 21 \cdot 15 \cdot 20}\), we will follow these steps:

1. **Calculate the product** of the numbers \(5\), \(10\), \(14\), \(21\), \(15\), and \(20\).
2. **Take the square root** of the resulting product.

### Step 1: Calculate the Product

We already calculated the product in the previous task:

\[
5 \cdot 10 = 50
\]
\[
50 \cdot 14 = 700
\]
\[
700 \cdot 21 = 14700
\]
\[
14700 \cdot 15 = 220500
\]
\[
220500 \cdot 20 = 4410000
\]

Thus, the product of the numbers is:

\[
P = 5 \cdot 10 \cdot 14 \cdot 21 \cdot 15 \cdot 20 = 4410000
\]

### Step 2: Take the Square Root

Now, we will take the square root of the product:

\[
\sqrt{4410000}
\]

To simplify this calculation, we can factor \(4410000\) into its prime factors or use a calculator. However, we can also break it down as follows:

\[
4410000 = 441 \times 10000
\]

Calculating the square roots separately:

1. \(\sqrt{441} = 21\) (since \(21^2 = 441\))
2. \(\sqrt{10000} = 100\) (since \(100^2 = 10000\))

Now, we multiply these results:

\[
\sqrt{4410000} = \sqrt{441} \cdot \sqrt{10000} = 21 \cdot 100 = 2100
\]

### Final Result

Thus, the final answer is:

\[
\sqrt{5 \cdot 10 \cdot 14 \cdot 21 \cdot 15 \cdot 20} = \boxed{2100}
\]
