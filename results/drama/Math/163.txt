To solve the problem, we will follow these steps:

1. **Calculate the original edge length of the cube using the surface area.**
2. **Determine the new edge length after doubling the original edge length.**
3. **Calculate the volume of the new cube using the new edge length.**

### Step 1: Calculate the Original Edge Length

Given the surface area \( S \) of the cube is 600 square inches, we use the surface area formula:

\[
S = 6a^2
\]

Setting \( S = 600 \):

\[
600 = 6a^2
\]

Now, divide both sides by 6:

\[
a^2 = \frac{600}{6} = 100
\]

Taking the square root of both sides gives us the original edge length \( a \):

\[
a = \sqrt{100} = 10 \text{ inches}
\]

### Step 2: Determine the New Edge Length

Now, we double the original edge length:

\[
L' = 2 \times a = 2 \times 10 = 20 \text{ inches}
\]

### Step 3: Calculate the Volume of the New Cube

Using the volume formula for a cube:

\[
V = s^3
\]

where \( s \) is the edge length. Substituting the new edge length \( L' \):

\[
V = (20)^3 = 8000 \text{ cubic inches}
\]

### Conclusion

The volume of the new cube, after doubling the edge length of the original cube with a surface area of 600 square inches, is:

\[
\boxed{8000} \text{ cubic inches}
\]
