To find the integer root of the polynomial 

\[
g(x) = x^3 - 14x^2 + 18x + 72,
\]

we can use the Rational Root Theorem, which suggests that any rational root, in this case an integer root, must be a divisor of the constant term (72).

The divisors of 72 are:

\[
\pm 1, \pm 2, \pm 3, \pm 4, \pm 6, \pm 8, \pm 9, \pm 12, \pm 18, \pm 24, \pm 36, \pm 72.
\]

We will test these values in the polynomial \( g(x) \) to find an integer root.

1. Testing \( x = 1 \):

\[
g(1) = 1^3 - 14 \cdot 1^2 + 18 \cdot 1 + 72 = 1 - 14 + 18 + 72 = 77 \quad (\text{not a root})
\]

2. Testing \( x = -1 \):

\[
g(-1) = (-1)^3 - 14 \cdot (-1)^2 + 18 \cdot (-1) + 72 = -1 - 14 - 18 + 72 = 39 \quad (\text{not a root})
\]

3. Testing \( x = 2 \):

\[
g(2) = 2^3 - 14 \cdot 2^2 + 18 \cdot 2 + 72 = 8 - 56 + 36 + 72 = 60 \quad (\text{not a root})
\]

4. Testing \( x = -2 \):

\[
g(-2) = (-2)^3 - 14 \cdot (-2)^2 + 18 \cdot (-2) + 72 = -8 - 56 - 36 + 72 = -28 \quad (\text{not a root})
\]

5. Testing \( x = 3 \):

\[
g(3) = 3^3 - 14 \cdot 3^2 + 18 \cdot 3 + 72 = 27 - 126 + 54 + 72 = 27 \quad (\text{not a root})
\]

6. Testing \( x = -3 \):

\[
g(-3) = (-3)^3 - 14 \cdot (-3)^2 + 18 \cdot (-3) + 72 = -27 - 126 - 54 + 72 = -135 \quad (\text{not a root})
\]

7. Testing \( x = 4 \):

\[
g(4) = 4^3 - 14 \cdot 4^2 + 18 \cdot 4 + 72 = 64 - 224 + 72 + 72 = -16 \quad (\text{not a root})
\]

8. Testing \( x = -4 \):

\[
g(-4) = (-4)^3 - 14 \cdot (-4)^2 + 18 \cdot (-4) + 72 = -64 - 224 - 72 + 72 = -288 \quad (\text{not a root})
\]

9. Testing \( x = 6 \):

\[
g(6) = 6^3 - 14 \cdot 6^2 + 18 \cdot 6 + 72 = 216 - 504 + 108 + 72 = -108 \quad (\text{not a root})
\]

10. Testing \( x = -6 \):

\[
g(-6) = (-6)^3 - 14 \cdot (-6)^2 + 18 \cdot (-6) + 72 = -216 - 504 - 108 + 72 = -756 \quad (\text{not a root})
\]

11. Testing \( x = 8 \):

\[
g(8) = 8^3 - 14 \cdot 8^2 + 18 \cdot 8 + 72 = 512 - 896 + 144 + 72 = -168 \quad (\text{not a root})
\]

12. Testing \( x = -8 \):

\[
g(-8) = (-8)^3 - 14 \cdot (-8)^2 + 18 \cdot (-8) + 72 = -512 - 896 - 144 + 72 = -1480 \quad (\text{not a root})
\]

13. Testing \( x = 9 \):

\[
g(9) = 9^3 - 14 \cdot 9^2 + 18 \cdot 9 + 72 = 729 - 1134 + 162 + 72 = -171 \quad (\text{not a root})
\]

14. Testing \( x = -9 \):

\[
g(-9) = (-9)^3 - 14 \cdot (-9)^2 + 18 \cdot (-9) + 72 = -729 - 1134 - 162 + 72 = -1953 \quad (\text{not a root})
\]

15. Testing \( x = 12 \):

\[
g(12) = 12^3 - 14 \cdot 12^2 + 18 \cdot 12 + 72 = 1728 - 2016 + 216 + 72 = 0 \quad (\text{is a root})
\]

Thus, the integer root of the polynomial \( g(x) \) is 

\[
\boxed{12}.
\]
