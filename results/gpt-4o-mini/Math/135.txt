To simplify the expression \( x^2 + 2x(5-x) + (5-x)^2 \), we will first expand each term.

1. Start with the term \( 2x(5-x) \):
   \[
   2x(5-x) = 2x \cdot 5 - 2x \cdot x = 10x - 2x^2
   \]

2. Next, expand the term \( (5-x)^2 \):
   \[
   (5-x)^2 = 5^2 - 2 \cdot 5 \cdot x + x^2 = 25 - 10x + x^2
   \]

Now, we can substitute these expansions back into the original expression:
\[
x^2 + 2x(5-x) + (5-x)^2 = x^2 + (10x - 2x^2) + (25 - 10x + x^2)
\]

Combine like terms:
- The \( x^2 \) terms:
  \[
  x^2 - 2x^2 + x^2 = 0
  \]
- The \( x \) terms:
  \[
  10x - 10x = 0
  \]
- The constant term:
  \[
  25
  \]

Putting it all together, we have:
\[
x^2 + 2x(5-x) + (5-x)^2 = 0 + 0 + 25 = 25
\]

Thus, the final result is:
\[
\boxed{25}
