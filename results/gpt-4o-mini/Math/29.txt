To simplify the expression \(\sqrt{(\sqrt{56})(\sqrt{126})}\), we start by combining the square roots:

\[
\sqrt{(\sqrt{56})(\sqrt{126})} = \sqrt{\sqrt{56} \cdot \sqrt{126}} = \sqrt{56 \cdot 126}
\]

Next, we calculate \(56 \cdot 126\). First, we can factor both numbers:

\[
56 = 2^3 \cdot 7
\]
\[
126 = 2 \cdot 3^2 \cdot 7
\]

Now, we multiply these factorizations together:

\[
56 \cdot 126 = (2^3 \cdot 7)(2 \cdot 3^2 \cdot 7) = 2^{3+1} \cdot 3^2 \cdot 7^{1+1} = 2^4 \cdot 3^2 \cdot 7^2
\]

Now we can compute the square root of this product:

\[
\sqrt{56 \cdot 126} = \sqrt{2^4 \cdot 3^2 \cdot 7^2}
\]

Using the property of square roots, we can simplify this further:

\[
\sqrt{2^4} = 2^2 = 4, \quad \sqrt{3^2} = 3, \quad \sqrt{7^2} = 7
\]

Thus, we have:

\[
\sqrt{2^4 \cdot 3^2 \cdot 7^2} = 4 \cdot 3 \cdot 7 = 84
\]

Now, we need to find \(\sqrt{56 \cdot 126}\):

\[
\sqrt{56 \cdot 126} = 84
\]

Since we need to express this in the form \(a\sqrt{b}\), we can write:

\[
84 = 84\sqrt{1}
\]

Here, \(a = 84\) and \(b = 1\). Now we compute \(a + b\):

\[
a + b = 84 + 1 = 85
\]

Thus, the final answer is:

\[
\boxed{85}
