To find the sum \( S = 1 + 2 + 3 + \cdots + 100 \), we can use the formula for the sum of the first \( n \) natural numbers:

\[
S_n = \frac{n(n + 1)}{2}
\]

In this case, \( n = 100 \). Plugging in the value, we have:

\[
S_{100} = \frac{100(100 + 1)}{2}
\]

Calculating \( 100 + 1 \):

\[
100 + 1 = 101
\]

Now substituting this back into the formula:

\[
S_{100} = \frac{100 \times 101}{2}
\]

Next, we calculate \( 100 \times 101 \):

\[
100 \times 101 = 10100
\]

Now, we divide by 2:

\[
S_{100} = \frac{10100}{2} = 5050
\]

Thus, the sum \( 1 + 2 + 3 + \cdots + 100 \) is 

\[
\boxed{5050}
\]
