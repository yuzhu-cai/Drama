To simplify the expression \((7b^3)^2 \cdot (4b^2)^{-3}\), we will handle each part separately.

First, we simplify \((7b^3)^2\):
\[
(7b^3)^2 = 7^2 \cdot (b^3)^2 = 49 \cdot b^{3 \cdot 2} = 49b^6.
\]

Next, we simplify \((4b^2)^{-3}\):
\[
(4b^2)^{-3} = (4^{-3}) \cdot (b^2)^{-3} = \frac{1}{4^3} \cdot \frac{1}{(b^2)^3} = \frac{1}{64} \cdot \frac{1}{b^6} = \frac{1}{64b^6}.
\]

Now we combine the two results:
\[
(7b^3)^2 \cdot (4b^2)^{-3} = 49b^6 \cdot \frac{1}{64b^6}.
\]

When multiplying these, we can simplify:
\[
49b^6 \cdot \frac{1}{64b^6} = \frac{49}{64} \cdot \frac{b^6}{b^6} = \frac{49}{64} \cdot 1 = \frac{49}{64}.
\]

Thus, the simplified expression is:
\[
\boxed{\frac{49}{64}}.
\]
